About Me

 

Anthony Ostuni

I am a PhD student in UC San Diego's CS theory group, where I am extremely fortuate to be advised by Daniel Kane and Shachar Lovett. I completed my undergraduate studies at the University of Maryland, College Park (UMD), receiving a BS in computer science and applied mathematics.

I've previously spent time as a researcher at UMD's Combinatorics and Algorithms for Real Problems REU program, as well as a software engineering intern at Google and the Johns Hopkins Applied Physics Laboratory.

I currently co-run UCSD Theory Lunch. I have previously assisted with visit days.

Research Interests

I am broadly interested in combinatorics and complexity. See Writing for my publications and other writing.
